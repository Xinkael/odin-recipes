Tinga

HOME

Description

Tinga is a typical Mexican dish made with shredded meat (chicken, pork, or beef) in a sauce made from tomato, onion, garlic, and chipotle pepper. It is usually consumed on a tostada, taco, quesadilla, or molote, accompanied by avocado, fresh cheese, chopped lettuce, cream, or radish.

It is a simple dish, very popular in the center of the country and very versatile; there are tinga dishes made with tuna, grouper, or other fish, and also vegetarian tinga recipes made with carrots, sweet potato, or mushrooms. In Morelos, for example, it is typical to eat tinga made with cazahuate mushrooms. In Edomex, you can find tinga made with green chorizo. In Tlaxcala, it usually includes chorizo or longaniza and potatoes. However, the most popular tinga in all of Mexico is chicken tinga. In some homes, chicken and beef or pork meats are mixed.

Ingredients

  • 3 saladet tomatoes, cut into quarters
  • 1 can of chipotle pepper
  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • 1 white onion, sliced
  • 1 clove of garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 Knorr® Swiss chicken broth cube
  • 1 bay leaf

Steps

  1. Blend the tomatoes with the chipotle and water. Strain and reserve.
  2. Heat the oil in a large pot and sauté the onion with the garlic until it is soft and transparent.
  3. Pour in the tomato broth, the Knorr® Swiss Chicken Broth cube, and let it season over medium heat for 5 to 10 minutes.
  4. Add the chicken, the bay leaf, and let it cook over medium heat for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Serve and accompany with corn tortillas or tostadas.
