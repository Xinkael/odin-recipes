Churros

Description

A cinnamon-sugar topped fried pastry dough stick, they're similar to doughnuts, but they have ridges because they are piped out of a pastry bag. Because they are fried instead of baked, churros have a fluffy and tender interior with a satisfyingly crispy exterior.

Ingredients

  • 800ml water
  • 200g butter
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 400g sifted flour
  • 4 eggs
  • 15g sugar
  • 5g salt

Steps

  1. Prepare the flavored water:
    Infuse a large cinnamon stick in 1 liter of water and bring it to a boil. Once boiled, strain it, but only use 800 milliliters. Precision matters.
  2. Preheat the mixing bowl:
    Use the same bowl that you'll knead the dough in. This helps keep the temperature steady, partially cooking the dough before adding flour.
  3. Combine the liquids and flavorings:
    Add the cinnamon water, butter, salt, sugar, and vanilla to the bowl. Bring to a boil to fully integrate the flavors.
  4. Incorporate the flour:
    Add the sifted flour to the boiling mixture. Stir constantly to fully integrate it, making sure there are no clumps to avoid splattering during frying.
  5. Cool slightly before adding eggs:
    Let the dough cool a bit to prevent the eggs from scrambling or changing the dough's texture.
  6. Prepare the eggs:
    Beat the eggs thoroughly before incorporating them into the dough.
  7. Mix in the eggs gradually:
    Add the eggs little by little, fully incorporating each addition before adding more.
  8. Knead the dough:
    Transfer the dough to a work surface and knead briefly. The dough should be soft but not sticky.
  9. Shape the churros:
    Use a piping bag with a 1M star tip to pipe long strips of dough and cut to size. A traditional churro press is not necessary for this soft dough.
  10. Fry the churros:
    Heat oil to 170–180°C (340–355°F). Fry the churros for about 5 minutes, or until golden and crispy.
  11. Serve and enjoy:
    Remove from the oil, drain, and enjoy your perfectly crispy churros!
